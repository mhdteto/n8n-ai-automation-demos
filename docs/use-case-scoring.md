# AI Automation Use Case Scoring

## Purpose

Not every business process should be automated first.

This scoring model helps prioritize SMB automation opportunities.

---

## Recommended scoring dimensions

| Dimension | Max score |
|---|---:|
| Manual time spent | 20 |
| Repetition level | 20 |
| Process clarity | 20 |
| Business impact | 20 |
| Risk level | 20 |
| **Total** | **100** |

---

## 1. Manual time spent - 20 points

| Situation | Score |
|---|---:|
| Takes several hours per week | 20 |
| Takes 1-2 hours per week | 10 |
| Takes little time | 5 |
| Rarely done | 0 |

---

## 2. Repetition level - 20 points

| Situation | Score |
|---|---:|
| Highly repetitive | 20 |
| Somewhat repetitive | 10 |
| Mostly custom | 5 |
| Never repetitive | 0 |

---

## 3. Process clarity - 20 points

| Situation | Score |
|---|---:|
| Clear rules and inputs | 20 |
| Mostly clear | 10 |
| Ambiguous | 5 |
| Undefined process | 0 |

---

## 4. Business impact - 20 points

| Situation | Score |
|---|---:|
| Direct revenue or customer impact | 20 |
| Operational efficiency impact | 10 |
| Minor convenience | 5 |
| Low impact | 0 |

---

## 5. Risk level - 20 points

For this dimension, lower risk means higher score.

| Situation | Score |
|---|---:|
| Low risk with human review | 20 |
| Moderate risk | 10 |
| High risk | 5 |
| High risk without review | 0 |

---

## Score interpretation

| Score range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 80-100 | Strong automation candidate |
| 60-79 | Good candidate with review |
| 40-59 | Needs process clarification |
| 0-39 | Not a good first automation |

---

## Best first use cases

Good first automation candidates are usually:

- repetitive;
- low-risk;
- easy to review;
- based on clear inputs;
- useful even with human approval;
- connected to sales, support, marketing or operations.
